WHAT WE
ANALYSE

The model considers a combination of demographic, socioeconomic, and academic factors to generate a reliable math score prediction.

👤
01
Demographic Profile

Gender and race/ethnicity are used as baseline demographic features that correlate with educational outcomes in the dataset.

🎓
02
Parental Education

A parent's highest level of education is one of the strongest socioeconomic predictors of student academic performance.

🍱
03
Lunch Type

Standard vs. free/reduced lunch serves as a proxy for household income and access to resources, influencing learning outcomes.

📝
04
Test Prep Course

Completion of a preparation course shows meaningful improvement in predicted scores across all groups in the training data.

📖
05
Reading Score

Reading ability is strongly correlated with mathematics performance. Students who read well tend to understand problem statements better.

✏️
06
Writing Score

Writing score reflects analytical thinking and structured reasoning — skills that directly transfer to mathematical problem solving.

Process
HOW IT
WORKS
01
Fill the Form

Enter the student's gender, ethnicity, parental education, lunch type, test prep status, and their reading and writing scores.

02
Preprocess

The inputs are encoded and scaled using the same preprocessor pipeline that was fitted during model training.

03
Model Inference

A trained regression model runs on the processed features to output a continuous predicted mathematics score.

04
View Result

The predicted score is displayed instantly along with a performance tier — Strong, Average, or Needs Support.
